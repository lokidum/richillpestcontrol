# Richill Pest Control

**Register:** brand. This is a one-page marketing site for a local service business. The design IS the product.

## Product purpose

A one-page conversion-focused website for Richill Pest Control, a third-generation family pest control business in Perth, Western Australia. Job: turn a Perth homeowner who just spotted a pest into a phone call or quote request to Mike. Secondary: rank well for "pest control Perth" and "termite inspection Perth" local searches.

## Users

- Perth homeowners aged 30 to 65 who just saw a redback, cockroach, mud trail, or a wasp nest in the eaves and want it sorted today. Most are on a mobile, often inside their own house.
- New Perth homebuyers needing a pre-purchase termite inspection before settlement.
- Property managers and real estate agents needing end-of-lease pest treatments with a signed report.
- Small business owners (cafes, strata, schools, aged care) needing scheduled commercial pest control with audit paperwork.

Around 60 to 70 percent of bookings happen on mobile, because pest control is reactive: someone sees something, they want it dealt with now.

## Brand voice

Warm, plain spoken, slightly old-school. Personal. Not corporate. The owner Mike talks like a real Perth tradesman who treats every home like his own. Australian English throughout (organise, colour, defence, centre, behaviour, analyse). No em dashes or en dashes anywhere. Phone formatting 0426 427 299 with spaces.

Reference lines in Mike's voice from his Google review replies:
- "When uninvited guests come to stay, I don't like to leave customers waiting if I can help it."
- "When there's little lions about, it's always best to know your options thoroughly."
- "It's a privilege to be entrusted with caring for your home and beautiful family."
- "I treat my customers home and family as if it were my own."

Talk to one person, not "our valued customers". Use specifics over adjectives. Lean on the three generations, the 1959 start year, the low tox specialty. Name Mike. Reviews already do this and customers want a name to put on the bloke at the door.

## Strategic positioning

A small Perth family business doing pest control for three generations, with safer treatments and honest conversations. Sits between the one-ute operators who can't get to you for two weeks and the national franchises that read off a script. Termite specialists, low tox options, owner answers the phone.

Average job value $250 (general pest), more for termite work. Currently 5.0 stars across 12 Google reviews, every review naming Mike personally.

## Anti-references

What this brand must NEVER feel like:

- **National franchises** (Tom's Pest Control style): corporate copy, "leading provider", "industry standard", 1300 numbers, packaged tier pricing, photos of men in hazmat suits, content that bleeds between cities.
- **The Stitch "Command Center" aesthetic** that was rejected earlier this build: military commands ("DEPLOY RESPONSE TEAM", "MIKE ONLINE"), neon green status dots, ticker tape urgency, "Rapid Callout". Too aggressive for a family tradie.
- **Stock pest macro shots**: close-ups of cockroaches and spiders. Gross, off-brand, and customers don't want them mid-scroll.
- **American-suburb photography**: porches with shingles, swing seats, anything that doesn't read as a Perth home.
- **Glassy SaaS dashboard chrome**: dark gradients with cyan accents, monospace status indicators, "live" badges, blurry hero gradients.
- **The hero-metric template**: big number on the left, supporting stats, gradient accent. SaaS cliche.
- **Identical card grids repeating endlessly.** The previous draft slipped into this with the "every pest" 35-tile wall. Cut.

## What I want users to feel

Two seconds in: "this is a real person, not a call centre." Five seconds in: "they've been doing this since before my parents were born." Ten seconds in: "the number's right there, I can ring." If the page reads warm, local, and unhurried, it has worked.
